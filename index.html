Project Overview

As my first project at the University of South Florida, I served as the Hardware Lead for a team of five students in the Foundations of Engineering Laboratory. Our mission was to design and build an engaging line-following robot to introduce robotics concepts to over 100 K-12 students. We created "Gary the Snail," a robot with a custom 3D printed chassis. The project was a massive success, winning 1st place out of 500 competing projects.

Project Breakdown

Hardware and 3D Printing

As Hardware Lead, I managed the integration of all electronic components, including motors, batteries, and sensors, onto a custom chassis which was topped with a 3D printed shell of "Gary" designed by our team.

Software and Control

The robot's movement was programmed in C++ on an Arduino. It uses two IR sensors that emit and detect infrared light.

IR Sensor Logic

When a sensor is over the black line, the light is absorbed and the sensor sends a LOW signal. Based on which sensor detects the line, the code adjusts the motor speeds to steer the robot and keep it centered on the path.
